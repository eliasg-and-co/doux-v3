Recorded music has been frozen since 1877.

doux is a new format where the recording responds to the world it plays in. Not by generating sound with an algorithm, but by modulating what was already composed, allowing for infinite variations of a finished track, remixed by the world around you.

Every note is fixed. The world around you never is.

On February 23rd, 2026, a blizzard hit New York. The L Train ran (understandably) terribly, turning crisp drums into a filtered, messy lurch. The 16 inches of snow that battered the city set low bit depths, slow filters, and heavy autowah on the lead. The city's paralysis wrote itself into the music in real time.


This is what that sounded like, and it will never sound exactly like that again.
